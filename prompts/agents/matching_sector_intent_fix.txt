# Matching Pipeline — Sector Intent Fix
# Decision log: May 2026
#
# Root cause incident: user with "AI implementation" background and PhD in
# Petroleum Engineering received 15/15 matches from Anthropic, 0 from oil and gas,
# despite explicitly stating "Seeking senior leadership role in oil and gas industry."
#
# Three root causes identified and fixed. This file documents the design decision
# for each fix so future sessions can understand why these rules exist.

---

## Root Cause 1 — build_intent_query excluded preferred_sectors and goals_text
# File: agents/profile_agent.py

### Problem
build_intent_query() previously excluded preferred_sectors and goals_text entirely.
The rationale was that sector/employer names "skew the embedding toward past experience."
This is correct for preferred_companies (employer name bias), but wrong for preferred_sectors
when the user's target industry differs from their skills background.

A petroleum engineer with "AI implementation" in their role_description gets an embedding
that points toward AI companies. Without preferred_sectors or goals_text in the query,
the pipeline had no signal that the user wants oil and gas, not tech.

### Fix
Include goals_text at MEDIUM weight and preferred_sectors as "Target industry: ..." at LOW weight.
Exclude preferred_companies (employer names still cause bias).

### Query weight hierarchy (current)
  HIGH:    role_description, title_include (repeated)
  MEDIUM:  seniority_level, work_modes, goals_text
  LOW:     preferred_sectors ("Target industry: oil and gas, energy")
  EXCLUDED: preferred_companies

### Why LOW for preferred_sectors (not MEDIUM or HIGH)?
Sectors are categorical labels. Embedding "oil and gas" at high weight would make the
profile embedding point strongly at oil and gas job descriptions, which is good for
ANN recall but may over-constrain similarity at the expense of finding adjacent roles.
LOW weight provides directional bias without locking the embedding to one sector cluster.

## Root Cause 2 — No per-company slot cap in _diversify()
# File: agents/filter_agent.py

### Problem
_diversify() capped results by SECTOR at 60% (max 9 of 15 from one sector).
But the sector cap was bypassed when <20% of jobs had sector data.
Even when active, one company could fill all 9 sector slots if its jobs
spanned multiple sector labels or had inconsistent sector tagging.

Result: Anthropic, which posts 100+ jobs across "AI", "Technology", "Research" sector
labels (or no sector at all), could fill all 15 candidate slots.

### Fix
Add _COMPANY_CAP = 2: no single company may occupy more than 2 of the 15 final slots.
This cap is ALWAYS enforced, regardless of sector data availability.
The sector cap continues to apply when sector data is sufficient.

### Constants (current)
  _COMPANY_CAP = 2             # hard cap per employer, always enforced
  _SECTOR_CAP_FRACTION = 0.60  # max 60% from one sector (when data available)
  _SECTOR_DATA_MIN_FRACTION = 0.20  # skip sector cap if <20% jobs have sector tagged

### Why 2 (not 1 or 3)?
1 per company would be too restrictive — a company with 50 relevant jobs
would be under-represented in candidates delivered to users who want that company.
3 is reasonable but still allows 3/15 = 20% from one employer.
2 is the right balance: provides variety, allows strong matches from a good company,
prevents dominance.

## Root Cause 3 — LLM had no explicit penalty for sector mismatches
# File: agents/match_agent.py — _build_system_prompt()

### Problem
The system prompt passed preferred_sectors as context ("Preferred sectors: oil and gas")
but gave no scoring rule for sector mismatches. The LLM would reason:
"This candidate has leadership experience. Anthropic needs a VP. Leadership transfers
across industries." Result: industry_alignment scored 0.55–0.70 for Anthropic
instead of near-zero.

### Fix
When profile.preferred_sectors is non-empty, inject a mandatory SECTOR RULE block
into the system prompt:

  SECTOR RULE (mandatory — applies before all other scoring):
  - This candidate's target sectors are: {sectors}
  - If the job's sector or company does NOT belong to any of these target sectors,
    score industry_alignment ≤ 0.25 regardless of other signals.
  - If the job clearly belongs to a target sector, score industry_alignment normally.

### Why ≤ 0.25 (not 0.0)?
Forcing 0.0 would be too strict. Some jobs are genuinely cross-sector
(e.g. a petroleum data scientist at an energy-focused AI company).
0.25 is a strong penalty that keeps mismatched jobs at the bottom of rankings
while allowing the LLM to give partial credit for adjacent cases.

### When does this rule fire?
Only when profile.preferred_sectors is non-empty. Users who have not set
sector preferences get the original unconstrained scoring — no regression risk.

---

## Important note for future sessions

"I do not want to work for Company X" written in profile text or goals_text
is NOT the same as adding Company X to excluded_companies.

excluded_companies is an array field in UserProfile. The hard filter checks this array.
Profile text is not parsed for company exclusions — it is only embedded.

Users must add companies to the Excluded companies field in Profile settings
for the hard filter to block those jobs entirely. Profile text exclusions
are soft signals that may affect embedding but do not guarantee filtering.
